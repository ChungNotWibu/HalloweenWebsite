About Halloween (31/10) :


● What is Halloween :


ㅤㅤ All Hallows' Evening (Halloween for short) is commonly known as the "Devil's Festival". In fact, Halloween is a traditional and special festival in the West, it is usually celebrated every year on October 31, before the Christian feast of All Saints. Halloween is the time to mark the end of the harvest season and the arrival of a cold winter. At the same time, this is also an opportunity for us to remember together about the Saints, Martyrs or loved ones who have passed away. To this day, Halloween has become a popular festival around the world, with a large / small scale depending on the country.


● The Origin of Halloween :

ㅤㅤ We already know what day Halloween is, but do you know the origin of this day? Actually, Halloween has been around 2000 years ago. This festival originated from the Celts, who lived in the lands of what is now Ireland, Great Britain and northern France. According to the religious beliefs of the Celts, they believed that on the eve of the new year, the boundary between the two worlds of yin and yang was very blurred, allowing the dead to return to the earthly realm. Therefore, they lit fires and dressed up in many strange shapes, to ward off evil spirits and welcome their lost loved ones back.


● Meaning of Halloween :

ㅤㅤ Looking at the origins of Halloween, we can see that this is more than just a ghost festival or a carnival. Halloween is also a sacred and important religious holiday, with many profound meanings such as:


Remembering and honoring the dead :

ㅤㅤ If someone asks you what day is Halloween, what does it mean? It can be answered that this is the first day (October 31) in the series of days honoring the saints (followed by All Saints' Day, November 1), and is also a time for us to remember those who have passed away. (Feast of All Souls November 2nd). According to legend, Halloween is the day when ghosts are allowed to return to earth to visit family members. If these souls receive many prayers from the earth, they will soon be baptized and return to God.


It is the day of exorcism and banishing evil things :

ㅤIf talking about the meaning of warding off ghosts and evil things on Halloween, we need to tell an Irish legend. The story is about a dead boy named Jack. Jack's soul cannot go to heaven, because when he was alive, he was a very stingy, selfish person who did not know how to share with others. However, this soul also cannot go to hell because in life, Jack also played with demons, so the demons did not want to catch him.
ㅤNot wanting to see the soul of their "friend" wandering and wandering like that, the demons took some embers in Hell and put them in the pumpkin, forming a lantern to help Jack light the way. that I go. To create more atmosphere to keep the fire, Jack also punched holes around the squash. That is why today, pumpkin has become the main symbol of this occasion.


● Fun activities on Halloween :

Masquerade Day :

ㅤWhen receiving the question of what day Halloween is, people will surely think of it as a masquerade day. This is understandable, because the act of dressing up as your favorite characters has become a feature of this festival.
ㅤDressing up in a strange shape originated with the original meaning of warding off evil spirits. But over thousands of years of development, people have gradually forgotten this meaning, instead, people prefer to see Halloween as a more eye-catching and impressive fashion show. On this occasion, everyone can enjoy dressing up as their favorite models, maybe cartoon characters, maybe the main actor in a certain movie, and have a fun and bustling parade on the street.

Pumpkin carving :

ㅤAfter reading the story about Jack's soul, you probably already understand what day Halloween is, why the pumpkin is a symbol of this day, right? Today, the activity of carving pumpkins during Halloween has gradually become more popular and creative. People can carve pumpkins into many different shapes, the most popular being the ghost face which is shaped into cute, childish expressions.

Knock on the door and ask for candy:

ㅤIf you ask Western children what day is Halloween, they will answer that it is the day to play the game "Trick or Treat". This custom began to be popular in the United States in the 1960s. On Halloween, children in the neighborhood often dressed up and invited each other to knock on doors to ask for candy. It is believed that these children are "playing" the returning souls, so giving candy also shows sharing for the souls of the deceased. If no candy is given, the children may play tricks and tease the host. However, to this day, this custom has gradually become more polite, the owners who want to give candy to the children will usually let them know in advance, and families who do not want to participate in this game will not be disturbed again.

Get Apples on Halloween:

ㅤThis custom originated with the Celtic people. When the land of this nation was occupied by the Romans, a part of Roman culture was also introduced and developed here, including the festival worshiping the goddess Pomona - the god of crops. According to legend, this goddess often hides in baskets of fruit, especially apples - a sacred fruit, often used to make offerings to the gods. Therefore, on Halloween, people often organize games related to apples such as taking apples in a pot of water, peeling apples, ... as a good luck ritual.

Organize parties

ㅤThe jubilant parties are indispensable on Halloween nights around the world. At these parties, you will have to transform into the characters you like or follow the "dresscode" set by the party owner. In Halloween parties, you will experience many extremely interesting games, as well as enjoy foods with bizarre shapes but still very eye-catching and impressive.

Halloween Food :

● Pumpkin soup

ㅤPumpkin is considered a typical symbol of Halloween, therefore, pumpkin dishes such as pumpkin soup, pumpkin pie, ... will be indispensable in this party. Each country and region in the world will have different ways of preparing pumpkin soup. Usually, this dish often has a fatty, slightly sweet taste, suitable for both children and adults.
ㅤTo get a really pure and delicious pumpkin soup, you can use a blender to puree the pumpkin first, then use a pressure cooker to cook.

● Soul cake

ㅤWhat day is Halloween? It is considered the day when the souls will be allowed to return to the earth, so the soul cake is also one of the dishes that need to appear on the Halloween table. Basically, this is a cookie with a round shape, raisins on top and decorated with a cross symbol. In addition, to create more atmosphere for the party, you can also make soul cakes with more "Halloween" shapes such as skulls. This cake is not too difficult to make, as long as you have a full set of household appliances such as an oven, whisk or beater.

● Barmbrack

ㅤBarmbrack is an Irish bread that contains dried fruits such as raspberries or grapes. On Halloween, people often put some more items inside this cake, including rings, coins and cloth. If you choose a cake with a coin, it means that you will have good fortune in the near future. On the contrary, the cloth will represent the difficulty that you need to face. Finally, the ring will be a symbol of love, predicting your upcoming love affair. This is also a very good game on this Halloween.

● Special Colcannon Mashed Potato

ㅤColcannon mashed potatoes also originate from Ireland, with the main ingredients being potatoes, milk, butter and a little kale. You can vary by adding pepper or bacon, making the meal more full of flavor and more attractive.

● Apple candy

You probably know that “Treat or Trick” is an indispensable game on every Halloween. People often choose sugar-coated apple candies to give to children on this occasion. The way to make this candy is also very simple, you just need to pick out big round apples and dip them in syrup or sugar, decorate with a layer of chocolate covered with nuggets is enough. Try making something for Halloween this year!
